FAQ

How is statutory notice calculated in the UK?
Statutory minimum notice is usually:
  • At least 1 week if employed between 1 month and 2 years
  • After 2 years: 1 week per full year of service (up to 12 weeks)
What if my contract notice is longer?
If your contract says a longer notice period than statutory minimum, you typically follow the contract. This tool lets you enter your contract notice (weeks) to reflect that.
Is the “last working day” always exact?
It’s an estimate based on weeks added to your notice date. Real last working day can depend on payroll cycles, holidays, company policy, or agreed early release.
Can my employer change my notice period?
Notice terms are usually set by your contract and statutory minimums. Changes typically require agreement. If you’re unsure, check your contract and speak with HR.
Do weekends and holidays count during a notice period?
A notice period is normally measured in calendar time (weeks). Your actual last working day may vary depending on company policy, agreed leave, or early release.
